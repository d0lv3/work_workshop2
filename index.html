Instructor Guide

The Splunk Investigation Queries

Instructor Note: "Usually, we search the 'Last 24 Hours'. But since we are hunting through historical logs, I am setting the time picker to All Time."
1. SSH Brute Force

The Initial Hunt (Finding the noise)

index="main" "Failed password"

The Aggregation (Who & Who targeted?)

index="main" "Failed password" | stats count by src_ip, user | sort - count

The Catch (Did they get in?)

index="main" "Accepted password" "192.168.1.100"
2. Malicious Payload

Hunting for the Payload

index="main" ("wget" OR "curl" OR "chmod" OR "payload.sh")

Final Incident Report View

index="main" ("wget" OR "chmod" OR "payload.sh") | table _time, host, _raw

The 5 Core Commands to Emphasize

  • index=
    The starting point. Tells Splunk which database to search.
  • | (The Pipe)
    Passes data from one command to the next.
  • stats count by
    Turns thousands of messy logs into a clean summary table.
  • sort - count
    Prioritizes data by putting the highest numbers at the top.
  • table
    Formats the final output to look professional for an Incident Report.
